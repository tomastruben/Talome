## First comment (pin it immediately)

```
Hey Product Hunt 👋 — I'm Tomas, the maker of Talome.

I built Talome because I was tired of self-hosting being a tax on evenings and weekends. Every platform I tried (CasaOS, Umbrel, TrueNAS) gave me a prettier button to click but still left me writing compose files, reading wikis, and debugging port conflicts by hand.

Talome takes a different approach: the AI *is* the interface. 230+ purpose-built tools give it real authority over Docker, the arr stack, Jellyfin, Plex, Home Assistant, Pi-hole, networking, backups, and more. Say "set up a media stack at /mnt/nas" and a minute later Jellyfin, Sonarr, Radarr, Prowlarr, and qBittorrent are running, wired together, and ready to use.

The most unusual thing it can do: modify its own TypeScript source code. Tell it the dashboard feels cluttered, and it reads the component, writes a diff, runs the type checker, and commits — rolling back automatically if anything breaks.

It's AGPL-3.0 (truly open source, not "community edition"), runs natively on Linux/macOS, needs Docker for managed apps, and asks you to bring your own Anthropic API key.

Happy to answer any questions — and huge thanks to everyone trying it out today. Feedback, bug reports, and rough edges all welcome.
```
